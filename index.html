A Biblical Story

Habakkuk was a prophet who was frustrated and waiting. He was crying out to God. Lord, are You even listening? Are You going to do anything? He climbed to his watch post, got still, and waited to see what God would say.

God answered him. Not with an explanation. With an instruction. Write it down. Engrave it plainly. What I am giving you is real. It is for an appointed time and it will not be late. Write it clearly enough that the one who picks it up can run with it.

Habakkuk did not have all the answers. He just had an assignment. So do you.

Two Paths

Habakkuk had a choice. He could stay in chapter one, frustrated, crying out, overwhelmed by what he could not understand or control. Or he could do what chapter two shows us he did. He climbed the wall. He got still. He positioned himself to hear. And when God spoke, he wrote it down.

Path One

It stays inside you.

The story you were meant to tell stays inside of you. Your testimony never has the impact it was intended to have. The book that was meant to encourage, equip, deliver, or set someone free never makes it to the printed page.

Path Two

You birth it.

You climb the wall like Habakkuk. You fast, pray, and listen. You surrender your fear and your timing to God. And with support, with a framework, and with others doing the same thing alongside you, you write it down and bring it to life.

Self Assessment

God honors your honesty. He already knows where you are. There is no judgment here, just an invitation to be real with yourself and with Him about what is inside you and what has been keeping it there.

Open your journal or the notes on your phone and answer these questions honestly.

Question 01

What has God put inside of me? What experience have I had that needs to be shared with the world? What situation, circumstance, season, or maybe even a trauma that I have overcome that God is calling me to write down so others can read it and be encouraged, equipped, delivered, set free, or maybe even have their life completely transformed? Write it down. Give it a name.

Question 02

On a scale of 1-10, how ready am I to birth this book? One being absolutely not ready. Ten being I am scared but yes, I am all in and ready to write my book.

Question 03

If my readiness to birth my book is low, why? What is holding me back? Is it the fear of not knowing how to do it? Or the fear of what others will think or say? Is it the fear of me not doing it right? Or is it the fear of me not having the money to invest in getting the guidance to write my book and publish it? Write down everything that comes to mind as reasons I am not all in to birth my book.

Question 04

If I do not write this book, who are those who will not be inspired, encouraged, equipped, delivered, or set free? Whose lives will never be transformed because I was unwilling to get uncomfortable and do the hard thing. Write my book. Tell my story.

(This is such an important question because it gets the focus off of you and onto those God wants to bless through your surrender and obedience to Him. Really meditate on this one. Ask God to give you a vivid picture of those who can be served by your book. Whose lives can be changed by reading your story.)

Take Action

I have just identified what God has placed inside of me, where I am in my readiness to birth it, what is holding me back, and who is waiting on the other side of my obedience.

About Your Coach

Tracie Bonds

Tracie Bonds is a Christian author, speaker, entrepreneur, and host of The Daily Prayer Call who helps believers learn how to practice hearing God's voice and applying His direction to their lives.

Her extraordinary journey from $232,000 in IRS debt and a home in foreclosure to earning more than $12 million over her seventeen-year network marketing career was not built on conventional business strategies. It was built on learning to hear and obey God's voice.

Before entering network marketing, Tracie worked as a luxury real estate agent serving professional athletes and high net worth individuals. When financial crisis struck and everything seemed impossible, she cried out to God for direction. What followed was a series of divine instructions, connections, and opportunities that transformed not only her own life but the lives of countless others.

For more than thirteen years, Tracie has hosted The Daily Prayer Call, a weekday gathering where believers come together to seek God and learn to hear His voice. This daily practice of prayer became the testing ground for the nine essential practices she teaches in GOD, I WANT TO HEAR YOU, the first book in the Radical Faith Series.

Today, through GOD STORY Coaching, books, retreats, workshops, and speaking events, Tracie helps believers clarify their God given vision and walk in bold obedience to God's voice.

Tracie lives in Franklin, Tennessee and enjoys spending time with her children and grandchildren, traveling, and documenting her own God stories.
